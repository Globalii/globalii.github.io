FAQs: Understanding Community Interpreting and GII

1. What is Global Interpreting Innovations (GII)?
GII is a professional organization that provides training for interpreters in community settings such as healthcare, education, and social services. We equip interpreters with the skills and knowledge needed to communicate effectively and ethically, ensuring equitable access for diverse communities.
2. What is community interpreting?
Community interpreting is a specialized profession where interpreters facilitate communication in settings like:
  • Healthcare: Hospitals, clinics, and medical appointments.
  • Education: Schools, parent-teacher conferences, and academic programs.
  • Social Services: Government assistance programs and community organizations.
In the past, family or friends were often used as interpreters. Today, professional interpreters are preferred and, in many cases, required due to legal, ethical, and professional standards.
3. Why is professional training important for interpreters?
Professional training ensures that interpreters:
  • Are proficient in both languages they interpret.
  • Understand cultural nuances and ethical standards.
  • Provide accurate, impartial, and effective communication.
Federal laws, hospital accreditation agencies, and professional standards advocate for using qualified or certified interpreters to ensure equitable and clear communication in critical settings.
4. What is the difference between a qualified and a certified interpreter?
Qualified Interpreters: Must complete professional training, such as a 40-hour course, and demonstrate language proficiency.
Certified Interpreters: Go a step further by passing a recognized certification exam from organizations like the National Board of Certification for Medical Interpreters (NBCMI) or the Certification Commission for Healthcare Interpreters (CCHI).
5. What is a 40-hour interpreter training course?
A 40-hour interpreter training course is widely regarded as the minimum standard for community interpreters. It provides foundational knowledge and skills in:
  • Interpreting techniques.
  • Ethics and standards of practice.
  • Language proficiency development.
6. What is The Community Interpreter® program?
The Community Interpreter® is a nationally recognized 40-hour training program tailored to aspiring community interpreters. It covers key topics, including:
  • Ethics and standards of practice.
  • Techniques for consecutive and simultaneous interpreting.
  • Cultural competence and professionalism.
GII is licensed to offer this program, ensuring participants receive high-quality training.
7. Who can take the 40-hour training course?
This course is ideal for:
  • Aspiring interpreters who are bilingual.
  • Professionals looking to meet qualifications for community interpreting.
  • Individuals starting a career in healthcare, education, or social services interpreting.
8. Why is community interpreting important?
Community interpreting plays a vital role by:
  • Ensuring equal access to services for individuals with limited English proficiency.
  • Supporting compliance with federal civil rights legislation and state laws.
  • Facilitating clear communication in crucial settings like healthcare and education.
9. How can I become a certified interpreter?
To become a certified interpreter:
  • Complete professional training, such as this 40-hour course.
  • Pass a recognized certification exam from an organization like NBCMI or CCHI.
10. What kind of jobs can I get after this course?
Completing this course prepares you for roles such as:
  • Community Interpreter: Work in healthcare, education, or social services.
  • Medical Interpreter: Work in hospitals or clinics.
  • Education Interpreter: Work in schools or academic programs.
  • Social Services Interpreter: Work in government or community programs.
  • Freelance (1099) Interpreter: Take jobs from multiple interpreting agencies as an independent contractor.
